Account Overview
9 June 2026   ·   Call — 35 min, with Lucía Ferrán (COO)
Good first qualification call. Lucía is sharp, ex-consulting, joined Pink Papaya 8 months ago to professionalise ops. She owns logistics, CX and finance. Founders (Marta and Jandro) stay on brand/product.
Pain is real and urgent. Their incumbent courier is "breaking" every time volume spikes. Last peak was a disaster — lots of parcels dumped at pickup points with no notice to the customer. Lucía said their Instagram DMs "turned into a customer service hotline" for two weeks.
Volumes: she threw out "around 3,500-4,000 parcels a day on a normal week, way more in drops and sales." Wants to firm this up with real data. Growing fast — roughly 4x year on year.
Geography: most of it is Spain, but she mentioned more than once they're "about to go big in France" and that whatever carrier they pick "needs to grow with us." Flagged this as important — need to dig into whether FR is in scope for day one or later.
Weekend: didn't ask for it directly, but when I asked when they ship most, she said "our sales spike Thursday to Sunday, the weekend is when everything happens." Worth pulling on this thread.
9 June 2026   ·   Internal note (post-call)
Gut feel: strong fit on the Spain home-delivery core. The peak story is exactly our sweet spot. Risk areas: France (we don't do it), possible PUDO ask, and I need to understand their parcel profile — if they're moving into bulky home goods that changes things.
Action: send service overview, set up follow-up with their data.
3 June 2026   ·   Inbound lead — webform + LinkedIn
Lucía reached out via the Amazon Shipping contact form and pinged me on LinkedIn the same day. Quote from the form: "We've outgrown our current courier and need a partner who won't collapse on us during sales season."
Mentioned they were referred by a contact who ships with us and is happy. Warm lead.
3 June 2026   ·   Desk research
Pink Papaya: founded early 2024 in Madrid. D2C fashion, very strong on social (combined ~600k followers across IG + TikTok). Mostly womenswear + accessories, some unisex. Price points €40-180, sweet spot around €95.
They do frequent "drops" (limited collections) that sell out in hours — these create sharp, short demand spikes on top of the seasonal peaks.
Spotted on their site: a small but growing "Papaya Home" line — candles, ceramics, small textiles, a few larger decor pieces. Worth checking parcel sizes; some of this could be bulky/heavy.
Reviews: Trustpilot sits around 3.4. Reading the negative ones, the overwhelming theme is delivery — "never arrived", "left at a shop 40 min away", "took 3 weeks to get a refund for a lost parcel." Delivery is clearly hurting the brand.

Hi Daniel,
Sorry for the late reply — it's been one of those weeks. Let me dump what I have, apologies if it's a bit messy. Shout if anything doesn't make sense.
Volumes. On a normal week we're doing about 3,800 parcels a day. During our big sale moments it's a different planet — last Black Friday we hit roughly six times a normal day and our courier simply couldn't cope. Drops are shorter but brutal: a collection can sell out in two hours and we'll ship a few thousand orders the next morning.
Geography. Spain is the bulk of it today — call it three quarters. The rest is split between France and Italy, which have been growing on their own through the website without us even pushing. Honestly France is becoming core for us — Marta wants it treated as a home market, not an afterthought, and whoever we work with has to be able to deliver there properly. Italy is smaller and we're more relaxed about it. We also have a trickle into Portugal but it's tiny.
Parcels. 90% of what we ship is apparel and accessories, so light and soft — most of it well under 2kg. The Papaya Home line is a small part of orders but some of it is chunkier (a few ceramic and textile pieces, the odd larger decor item). Nothing enormous, but I wouldn't want to pretend it's all featherweight.
Delivery type. The vast majority of our customers want it delivered to their door — that's the Pink Papaya experience and what the brand is about. That said, our current courier has been pushing pickup points hard, and a chunk of our customers have ended up using them, mostly because home delivery kept failing. I'd love to understand what you'd recommend here.
The real issue. I'll be blunt about why we're talking to you. Three things are killing us:
Peak. When volume spikes, our courier drops parcels at random pickup points without telling anyone. Customers blame us, not them. Last peak our refund rate tripled and our DMs were chaos.
Claims. When something is lost or damaged, it takes them the best part of a month to pay us back, and they ask for a mountain of paperwork every time. With our cash position that genuinely hurts.
Their customer service. It's slow and impersonal, so every delivery problem lands back on my tiny CX team. We're spending hours a day chasing "where is my order" on behalf of a courier we pay.
What I care about most is that our customers actually get their parcel, on time, at home, even when we're slammed — and that when something goes wrong it's sorted fast without my team having to fight for it. If you can show me that, you have my attention.
One more thing — we're not in a rush to rip everything out, but I'd want a partner who can scale into France with us over the next year. That's a real factor in who we pick.
Let me know what else you need.
Best,
Lucía Ferrán
COO, Pink Papaya
